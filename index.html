R&D & Optical Innovation System

User Research · Optical Design · Human Ergonomics

Our professional R&D team consists of optical engineers, ergonomic product designers and ophthalmic health researchers. We collect tens of thousands of real student study scenarios to optimize lamp height, light angle, spectrum parameters and clamping structure, eliminating the common defects of traditional desk lamps such as stroboscopic light, high blue light and uneven illumination.

Every prototype undergoes hundreds of brightness, color temperature and spectrum tests before mass production to ensure stable eye-friendly performance under long-term use.

Scene Simulation & Light Effect Calibration

Our laboratory simulates various indoor light environments such as cloudy daytime, evening dim light and bedroom night light to repeatedly calibrate the light output of each lamp model, ensuring consistent soft light effect under different ambient brightness.

AI Adaptive Intelligent Dimming Principle

A high-precision built-in light sensor captures real-time ambient light intensity. The internal microchip analyzes the brightness difference and automatically adjusts the lamp’s luminous flux and color temperature in real time without manual operation.

The lamp brightens automatically on dark cloudy days and turns soft warm light at night, greatly reducing eye burden during long reading and homework sessions.

Strict Multi-Dimensional Quality Inspection Standard

All finished lamps pass three major professional testing procedures: brightness uniformity test, full-spectrum blue light analysis, and 720-hour continuous aging stability test.

All optical indicators fully comply with national standards for student reading lamps, reaching RG0 zero-level low blue light and zero visible stroboscopic standard, suitable for long-term use by primary and secondary students, postgraduate candidates and office staff.
